**Title: Variance Budgeting: Decomposing Systematic and Idiosyncratic Risk in FTSE 100 Energy Sub-Sectors**

This research employs a variance decomposition framework to isolate the contribution of Integrated Oil & Gas majors (SHEL.L, BP.L, SSE.L, CNA.L) versus North Sea-focused E&P pure-plays (ITH.L, HBR.L, ENOG.L) to the total realized volatility of the FTSE 100 energy cohort. By calculating the daily rolling realized variance for each constituent and aggregating them into sub-sector indices, the model quantifies the proportion of sector-wide risk attributable to idiosyncratic E&P shocks versus diversified utility and integrated volatility. To address the absence of dividend-adjusted data, the analysis utilizes a robust M-estimator for variance calculation, which downweights the impact of discrete price jumps (ex-dividend events) that would otherwise inflate volatility estimates in a short 62-day sample. The study maps the "volatility budget" of the sector, identifying specific temporal regimes where E&P-specific tail risk dominates the broader energy price action. This objective provides a statistically sound descriptive profile of risk concentration, moving away from spurious predictive modeling to a rigorous characterization of current energy sector instability.
